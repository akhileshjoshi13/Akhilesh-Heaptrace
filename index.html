Root Cause Analysis
  • 01

    Product-Market Fit Failure on Client Side

    The client's product is not gaining market traction. This is the primary root cause — when a product doesn't sell, founders and leadership become internally focused on fundraising, pivoting, or cost reduction. External engagements like development partnerships are the first to be deprioritized or cut.

  • 02

    Avoidance Behavior Driven by Uncertainty

    The client is sending positive emails while avoiding live calls — a classic avoidance pattern. They may not yet have made a decision but are uncomfortable having a conversation they aren't ready for. The "everything is going good" emails are a delay tactic, not genuine status updates.

  • 03

    Absence of Proactive Renewal Conversation

    There has been no formal discussion initiated (from either side) about scope, renewal, or post-June roadmap. In client engagements, silence on renewals almost always means the client is not planning one. This conversation should have been initiated 4–6 weeks ago.
